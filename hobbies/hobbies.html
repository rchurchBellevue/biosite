Hobbies and Interests

An Appreciation of Literature

With the benefit of great teachers in high school, Jeni developed a love of reading, be it fiction or non-fiction. To say she is an avid reader is an understatement. In 2023 alone she's nearing 90 books read or listened to. You can follow along with her progress on her 2023 reads list on Pinterest.

For Jeni, a fiction story is a way to be transported to another world, another time, and another perspective. Non-fiction allows her to learn something new or to experience something from a different perpective

Favorite Work of Fiction

To Kill a Mockingbirdby Harper Lee is a classic work that's read by many a high-school student. For Jeni, it's her favorite work of fiction. In her words, it's a very relatable story of growing up and learning some truths about the world. But perhaps the key takeaway, is the idea of doing what's right, even if it's not the popular opinion

“The one thing that doesn’t abide by majority rule is a person’s conscience.”--Atticus Finch

This idea has stuck with Jeni and remains one of the many reasons she loves the book

Favorite Non-Fiction Work

When working at Half-Price Books if someone wanted something unique to read, Jeni would recommend Stiff: The Curious Life of Human Cadavers by Mary Roach. The book goes into a study of how bodies donated to science are utilized in a variety of fields, while also providing scientific insight to what happens to the physical body after one dies..

Jeni feels the author does a great job of detailing the facts of the biology of the decomposition process while also maintaining a respectful attitude.

DIY Projects Around the House

With her artistic ability, Jeni has found an outlet in using her creative skills around the house. Whether it's doing the work herself or consulting with a contractor to implement her designs, she has come up with many unique ways to improve the house.

The Kitchen Penny Backsplash

One of the first large scale projects taken on was something to spruce up the kitchen walls after a remodel. There are many trending designs for using things like glass tile, stone, or other materials, but Jeni wanted to find a different approach.

Realizing the cost of materials, she happened upon the idea of using copper pennies which when washed would provide a nice metallic contrast to the rest of the kitchen. A trip to the bank and quite a few rolls of pennies later the project was started. Utilizing grout, the pennies were secured to the kitchen walls and then polished to make sure they looked their brightest. The result is a lower cost and a quirky design for the kitchen.

Cat Calendar Mod Podge Bench

After a recent remodel we had a new "mud bench" in our front hallway to store shoes and other items. Although the original pine color was nice, Jeni wanted to jazz it up a bit. Using some old images from a FatCatArt calendar, an idea popped into her head as to how to put a personal touch on the bench.

p>

After staining the bench, she arranged the images in the desired locations, and then applied a product known as Mod-Podge to the images. This product acts as a combination glue, waterproofer, and sealer, so the images would be secure and have a layer of protection from any spills. After applying over ten coats, the bench was finally done and is quite impressive.

Puzzles

When the Covid-19 epidemic hit in 2020, a lot of people were stuck at home needing something to fill their time. Although Jeni had always enjoyed doing jigsaw puzzles as a child, she really embraced the activity during the pandemic and beyond.

For her, puzzles are a good way to keep her mind active and to provide a sense of accomplishment when one has been completed. Perhaps the only downfall is our cats seem to want to help with the puzzles which can make the process difficult.
