An Appreciation of Literature

With the benefit of great teachers in high school, Jeni developed a love of reading, be it fiction or non-fiction. To say she is an avid reader is an understatement. In 2023 alone she's nearing 90 books read or listened to. You can follow along with her progress on her 2023 reads list on Pinterest.

For Jeni, a fiction story is a way to be transported to another world, another time, and another perspective. Non-fiction allows her to learn something new or to experience something from a different perpective

Favorite Work of Fiction

To Kill a Mockingbirdby Harper Lee is a classic work that's read by many a high-school student. For Jeni, it's her favorite work of fiction. In her words, it's a very relatable story of growing up and learning some truths about the world. But perhaps the key takeaway, is the idea of doing what's right, even if it's not the popular opinion

“The one thing that doesn’t abide by majority rule is a person’s conscience.”--Atticus Finch

This idea has stuck with Jeni and remains one of the many reasons she loves the book

Favorite Non-Fiction Work

When working at Half-Price Books if someone wanted something unique to read, Jeni would recommend Stiff: The Curious Life of Human Cadavers by Mary Roach. The book goes into a study of how bodies donated to science are utilized in a variety of fields, while also providing scientific insight to what happens to the physical body after one dies..

Jeni feels the author does a great job of detailing the facts of the biology of the decomposition process while also maintaining a respectful attitude.
