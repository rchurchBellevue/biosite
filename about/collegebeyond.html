College and Beyond

  • Jeni was a triple major in college, pursuing degrees in English, Art and education.
  • In order to complete the required classes, she also attended classes during summer break
  • Each year she would either have observation or student teaching as part of her education coursework
  • Senior service project connected her with a nonprofit she still volunteers with 20 years later.

Other Highlights from college

  • Only a week or so into school she met her future husband
  • Was editor of the University literary journal The Ariel
  • Stint as a cartoonist for the university newspaper

A change of plans

During her student teaching assignment senior year, Jeni became aware that the idea she had in her head of teaching and the reality had become starkly different since high school. With classroom overcrowding, an increased workload, and a focus on meeting testing guidelines, it was a far cry from her high school teachers who could devote time to all students and create a positive working relationship.

Entering the workforce

Although she interviewed for a few positions, she ultimately chose not to pursue an education career. So what to do? She set out with a goal for the positions she was looking for and entered the workforce

She has held several positions since college, but in each one she's looked for a sense of purpose and the opportunity to help others. This has led to positions in retail, healthcare, and nonprofit work. One of the highlights was her time at the Kentucky Humane Society

Her career goals

I wanted to find a career where I felt useful, where I would look forward to going to each day, and which enabled me to put my skill set to use.

Kentucky Humane Society

Although she never followed her childhood dream of becoming a veterinarian, With her love of animals, working at the Kentucky Humane Society for years was a perfect match. She spent several years as an adoption counselor, which allowed her to connect adopting families with the perfect cat or dog for their household. As seen in the picture, sometimes those perfect matches for cats happened to be with her family.

In addition to her time in the adoptions department, she also served for several years in the community relations and fundraising department. Though not as hands on with animals, this did allow her the opportunity to connect with donors and volunteers to help keep money coming in to support the good work the KHS was doing. One of the highlights of this was an "Art for the Animals" event she organized with artists decorating banks to help collect donations for the organization.

Volunteering with Animal Rescue

Although her jobs have changed, something that has always been part of her adult life is volunteering. With her love of cats, she has been with a local rescue group since 2003, the Animal Protection Association in Jeffersonville, Indiana. website

She has fostered/adopted several animals from the shelter, as well as assisting with planning fundraising activities to allow the group to support it's adoptable cats. She even served as president of the group during the difficult time of the Covid-19 Pandemic.

She also works to educate people on the adoptability of cats with FIV(Feline Immunodeficiency Virus). This disease has a negative stigma around it, even though cats can live long healthy lives with the condition. She has even designed a tattoo for herself that shows her support for these special cats.
